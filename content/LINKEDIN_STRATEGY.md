# LinkedIn Content Strategy — Reference (Text-Only, Weekly Batch)

> Scope note: full source strategy assumes video/carousels + daily posting. This version is trimmed to what applies to **7 text posts, written in one weekly batch, spaced across the week via LinkedIn's native post scheduler.**

## Weekly Mix (7 posts)
- **2 Growth** — personal story / behind-the-scenes / opinion that gets strangers onto the profile
- **3 Nurture** — breakdown, framework, or opinion that builds authority (this is your strongest lane per the swipe-file data — lean into named frameworks + direct-address hooks here)
- **2 Conversion** — case-study style post or a specific result/lesson that showcases outcomes

Assign each of the 7 posts to one pillar so the week doesn't skew all-nurture or all-growth.

## Per-Post Rules
- **Hook:** direct address to the reader, not "I found/reviewed X" (see LINKEDIN_SWIPE_PATTERNS.md — this alone nearly doubled CTR in our own data)
- **No links in the post body** — LinkedIn suppresses reach on posts with outbound links. If a post needs a link, note "[link goes in first comment]" instead.
- **CTA:** always end with a concrete action or a principle/opinion restatement. Never "DM me if interested" — collapses trust.
- **Sentence rhythm:** vary sentence length; avoid dense uniform paragraphs — reads as low-effort and gets skipped.
- **Length:** 165–215 words has worked well for this audience; stay in that range unless the format clearly calls for shorter.

## Engagement Notes (for you, not the writing itself)
- Likes are vanity — the doc's sources weight **saves** and **comments/DM shares** as the real signal. Doesn't change how the post is written, but don't judge a post's success by like count alone.
- First 60 minutes after posting matters most for reach — reply to comments quickly. (Manual task, not something the routine handles.)

## Deferred (not in scope yet — revisit if cadence increases)
- Carousels, short-form video, infographics — all noted as top-performing formats in the source strategy, but out of scope while the routine is text-only.
- Daily engagement (commenting on others' posts) — a manual habit, not something this routine does.
